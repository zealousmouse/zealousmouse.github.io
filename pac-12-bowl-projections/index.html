Pac-12 bowl projections: The Apple Cup, Iron Bowl and other games that could impact Oregon and Utah’s playoff drive

admin | November 12, 2019 | College Sports | No Comments

All eye are on Oregon and Utah this month as the division leaders churn toward a showdown in the Pac-12 championship game, with a playoff berth potentially at stake.

While contemplating scenarios for the Ducks and Utes, the Hotline’s crack research staff wondered what other games could impact the fate of a one-loss Pac-12 champ in a resume showdown with other contenders.

Let’s assume the end-game isn’t clear for the selection committee — that the options aren’t as obvious as undefeated champions from the other leagues (Clemson, LSU, Ohio State and Baylor).

Then what?

How would a one-loss Pac-12 winner match up with a one-loss champ/runner-up from the Big 12, Big Ten or SEC?

Three results could be critical for Oregon/Utah in committee discussions following the Power Five championship games:

— Alabama-Auburn.

An Alabama victory, and especially a decisive Alabama victory, would support the case for slotting the Crimson Tide ahead of Oregon based on results against a common opponent.

Alabama wouldn’t have a division or conference title to match Oregon’s hardware, but the Tide would have a head-to-head victory on the road over a team that beat the Ducks on a neutral field.

For committee members inclined to favor the Tide over the Ducks, comparative results against Auburn would provide compelling justification.

The Pac-12 needs Auburn to win the Iron Bowl.

— A handful of games within the Pac-12, but not featuring Utah or Oregon, could prove impactful.

The selection committee has often used victories against ranked teams as a means of differentiating between playoff contenders.

If the Ducks or Utes are competing with one-loss Alabama or Oklahoma, each team’s record against ranked opponents could provide clarity.

If nothing changes, the Pac-12 champion would have one win over a ranked opponent — the victory in the conference title game.

The Pac-12 needs that to change.

It needs a third team (beyond Utah and Oregon) to be ranked in the committee’s top-25.

There’s only one path: An 8-4 team somehow sneaks in.

USC, Washington and Arizona State are the only teams capable of finishing 8-4, although for the Sun Devils to get there, they would have to beat Oregon, thereby eliminating the Ducks.

In our view, the team with the best chance to claim a spot low in the final rankings — based on current resume and remaining schedule — is Washington.

If the Huskies win out, they would finish with three consecutive victories, close losses to Utah and Oregon, and a win over USC.

In this scenario, UW’s upcoming dates with Colorado and Washington State could be significant as potential resume support mechanisms for the one-loss champion.

Could Oregon/Utah’s fate with the selection committee depend on the outcome of the Apple Cup?

It won’t be that simple, unless it is.

To the projections …

Bowl: Rose
Team: Oregon (8-1/6-0)
Home games left (two): Arizona, Oregon State
Road games left (one): Arizona State
Comment: A few too many obstacles remain for us to jump the Ducks into the playoff, but they’re making progress each week relative to the competition … even when they don’t play.

Bowl: Cotton
Team: Utah (8-1/5-1)
Home games left (two): UCLA, Colorado
Road games left (one): Arizona
Comment: The committee ranking (No. 8) was a tad higher than we expected and places the Utes in solid position for a berth in the Cotton were they to suffer a close loss in the Pac-12 championship.

Note: At-large slots for the New Year’s Six are based on top-25 ranking, unless a team is serving the role of replacement participant in a conference-affiliated game (because the champion has qualified for the CFP).

Bowl: Alamo
Team: USC (6-4/5-2)
Home games left (one): UCLA
Road games left (one): Cal
Comment: Current projections call for the Trojans to be the best option for the Alamo, whether they’re 7-5 or 8-4. And if they have a two-game advantage in conference record over all available teams, the Alamo would be required to invite them.

Bowl: Holiday
Team: Washington (6-4/3-4)
Home games left (one): Washington State
Road games left (one): Colorado
Comment: Win out, and the Huskies could climb as high as the Alamo (if the conference sends two to the New Year’s Six and USC loses once) or as low as the Redbox (if the conference sends one to the NY6 and USC doesn’t lose).

Bowl: Redbox
Team: Cal (5-4/2-4)
Home games left (one): USC
Road games left (two): Stanford, UCLA
Comment: Bowl officials would be happy to have the Bears. And after their October, the Bears would be happy to have the Redbox. Heck, they would be happy to have Shreveport.

Bowl: Sun
Team: Arizona State (5-4/2-4)
Home games left (two): Oregon, Arizona
Road games left (one): Oregon State
Comment: The Territorial Cup should be ASU’s sixth win (if the Devils haven’t already cleared the bar). And if the Wildcats win one of their next two, the rivalry could be make-or-break for both teams.

Bowl: Las Vegas
Team: Stanford (4-5/3-4)
Home games left (two): Cal, Notre Dame
Road games left (one): Washington State
Comment: We’ve watched Cal and Notre Dame lose too many games at Stanford Stadium — neither has won there this decade — to pronounce the Cardinal home for the holidays.

Bowl: Cheez-It
Team: Washington State (4-5/1-5)
Home games left (two): Stanford, Oregon State
Road games left (one): Washington
Comment: Three games to win two … but really two games to win two.
